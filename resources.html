📚 Career Theories & Models

Explore foundations of career guidance based on psychology and vocational behavior, These theories provide the foundation for career counseling using assessments and activities.

  • Strong Interest Inventory (Wikipedia)
  • Holland Codes / RIASEC Theory
  • MBTI (Myers-Briggs Type Indicator) – Personality-focused career guidance.
  • Super’s Life-Span Theory – Career development across life stages.
  • Ginzberg’s Developmental Theory – Career choices evolve from childhood to adulthood.
  • Big Five Personality Theory – Links traits to workplace behavior.
  • Kolb’s Experiential Learning – Hands-on activity-based learning cycle.
  • Gardner’s Multiple Intelligences – Recognizes varied learning and career styles.
  • Savickas’ Career Construction Theory – Careers shaped by personal stories and reflection.
  • Krumboltz’s Social Learning Theory – Careers influenced by learning and chance events.
  • Parsons’ Trait & Factor Theory – Match traits to job requirements.
  • Social Cognitive Career Theory (SCCT) – Role of confidence and goals in choices.
  • Person–Environment Fit Theory – Fit between you and your work setting.
  • Chaos Theory of Careers – Embrace change and unpredictability.
  • Planned Happenstance Theory – Use chance events to your advantage.
  • Theory of Work Adjustment – Fit between your values and job rewards.
  • Career Anchors Theory – Understand your guiding motivators.
